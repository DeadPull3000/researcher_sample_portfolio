Rethinking Layperson & Expert Relations for Digital Times.

My research examines how in the digital age, laypersons’ access to diverse sources of information and voice transforms layperson-expert relationships. I study how professional authority is constructed, challenged, and redefined as laypersons make their own knowledge claims, that may run counter to professional paradigms. Through empirical and conceptual analyses of layperson-expert interactions, I investigate how information becomes actionable knowledge, leading to both collaborative opportunities and polarizing tensions.
